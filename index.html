Values

Our shared background is in the retail/wholesale Bicycle/Outdoor industry where like the IT industry diversity is lacking. Projects/initiatives/companies are more successful when diverse talent is present and engaged. We believe that a partnership model is more profitable, diversity promotes better ideas and solutions, and practicing good corporate responsibility is an expectation in the 21st Century.
To that end Origma exists to diversify the IT and Outdoor industries. We believe that a path to success in North America's service economy can be drawn through the relatively blue collar Outdoor industry or the relatively white collar IT industry. Origma has committed 15% of staff time and 15% of profits to Open Source Software projects and organizations/initiatives who introduce computer science to students from underserved communities.

Our Process

Strategy

Every good recipe is a list of steps that have been perfected over time. We take on projects ranging in complexity from "ants on a log" simple to "four tier wedding cake" complex.
All projects start with a research phase where requirements, features, and milestones are defined. We use this time to understand vendor/client/customer challenges and align our priorities as partners.

Development and Design

Using project resource as a reference we use wireframes create our first project prototype. This may range from a very rough outline of the project to a product that is ready for user acceptance testing.
The IT inclined are known for putting their heads down in a dark office but there's more to software development than simply code. Our development sprints are only one week long** with feedback requested at any time. A week long sprint allows us to respond quickly to any changes/additions, and keeps our clients engaged in the development of the end product.

**depending on feature complexity

The inspiration came from our experience in the bicycle industry where new product is often named after/inspired by the natural world.
